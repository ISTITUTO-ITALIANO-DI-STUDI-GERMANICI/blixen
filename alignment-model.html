DiScEPT Alignment Model

The DiScEPT pipeline packages each bilingual alignment as a teiCorpus that gathers the shared scholarly metadata for the bundle and exposes alignment intelligence in a single standOff hub. Translation correspondences are recorded through ordered join cascades, whose @target attributes point to token identifiers distributed across the witnesses. The @type flag allows separate layers—linguistic alignment, structural cues, or commentary—to coexist without conflating their analytical purpose. A concluding linkGrp type="translation" threads together complementary join sets so scripts and viewers can traverse parallel passages without inspecting the witnesses directly.

Inside the corpus wrapper, each language is delivered as an autonomous TEI document. Paragraph-level <div> and <p> containers retain human-readable structure, while every lexical unit is tokenised into a <w xml:id="…"> element. These stable identifiers guarantee round-tripping between the alignment tables and the diplomatic transcription, empower granular annotation, and underpin automatic rendering in the alignment viewer. Because segmentation is expressed through token grouping rather than hard-coded sentences, editors can experiment with alternative readings, layer new analytical joins, or stage additional languages without rewriting the base texts. The public toolkit, examples, and deployment guidelines live in the DiScEPT repository at https://istituto-italiano-di-studi-germanici.github.io/DiScEPT/.
